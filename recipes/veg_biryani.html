Veg Biryani



Description

Veg Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originates from the Indian subcontinent. It is a popular vegetarian variation of the traditional biryani, known for its rich blend of fragrant spices and colorful assortment of vegetables. The dish is a harmonious blend of long-grain basmati rice, fresh vegetables like carrots, peas, cauliflower, and bell peppers, along with aromatic herbs and spices such as cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, and saffron. The rice is cooked with a delectable combination of spices, giving it a distinct and tantalizing flavor. Each bite of veg biryani offers a burst of flavors and textures, with the rice grains being perfectly cooked and infused with the essence of the spices, and the vegetables adding a refreshing and wholesome element to the dish.

One of the key highlights of veg biryani is its enticing aroma that fills the air as it cooks. The layers of spices and vegetables come together to create a heavenly fragrance that entices the senses. The dish is typically cooked using the traditional dum cooking method, where the rice and vegetable mixture is layered and slow-cooked in a sealed pot, allowing the flavors to meld together and intensify. The result is a visually appealing dish, with the rice grains stained with vibrant colors from the spices and vegetables. Veg biryani is not just a delight for the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes, making it a popular choice for special occasions, festivals, and celebrations. Whether enjoyed on its own or accompanied by raita (yogurt sauce) or curry, veg biryani is a hearty and satisfying vegetarian dish that showcases the rich culinary heritage of India.

Ingredients

  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, cut lengthwise into 1/4-inch thick strips
  • 5 fresh curry leaves
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 cup uncooked basmati rice
  • ½ cup fresh green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • ½ cup frozen cut green beans, thawed
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups water
  • ½ cup chopped cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat until it shimmers. Cook onion and curry leaves, stirring occasionally, until onion is dark brown, 12 to 15 minutes. Add tomato and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 3 to 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, soak rice in water to cover for 10 minutes. Drain well.
  3. Add beans, carrots, peas, potato, chile powder, biryani masala powder, and salt to onion mixture. Cook, stirring occasionally, 2 minutes. Stir in drained rice and water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and cook, covered, until water is absorbed, about 30 minutes. Stir in cilantro and let stand, covered, 10 minutes before serving.
