Chana Masala



Description

Chana masala is a vibrant and flavorful Indian dish that showcases the harmonious blend of spices and the rich taste of chickpeas. This vegetarian delight has its roots in North Indian cuisine and has gained popularity worldwide for its aromatic and robust flavors. The star ingredient, chickpeas, is simmered in a thick, tangy tomato-based gravy infused with an array of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. These spices lend depth and complexity to the dish, creating a perfect balance between heat and earthiness. The result is a hearty and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with fluffy basmati rice, naan bread, or roti.

The flavors of chana masala are further enhanced with the addition of onions, garlic, and ginger, which provide a savory base for the dish. The tanginess of tomatoes complements the spices, while a hint of lemon juice adds a refreshing zing. The dish often incorporates fresh cilantro for a burst of freshness and a touch of green color. Chana masala is not only delicious but also nutritious, as chickpeas are an excellent source of plant-based protein and dietary fiber. Its versatility allows it to be served as a main course, a side dish, or even as a filling for wraps or sandwiches. Whether enjoyed in the comfort of your home or at an Indian restaurant, chana masala is a culinary delight that tantalizes the taste buds and showcases the richness of Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped, or more to taste
  • 1 green chile pepper, seeded and chopped (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 pinch salt to taste
  • Â½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • water as needed
  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas
  • 1 teaspoon fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish, or more to taste

Steps

  1. Grind onion, tomato, ginger, garlic, and chile pepper together in a food processor into a paste.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry bay leaves in hot oil until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Pour the paste into the skillet and cook until the oil begins to separate from the mixture and is golden brown in color, 2 to 3 minutes. Season the mixture with chili powder, coriander, gram masala, turmeric, and salt; cook and stir until very hot, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Stir enough water into the mixture to get a thick gravy; bring to a boil and stir chickpeas into the gravy. Reduce heat to medium and cook until the chickpeas are heated through, 5 to 7 minutes. Garnish with cilantro.
